## Problem Statement
Retail clothing stores often rely on manual or partially computerized processes to manage:
- Sales transactions
- Inventory tracking (sizes, colours, stock levels)
- Customer records
- Employee management

These manual processes lead to:
- Stock inaccuracies and over/under-selling
- Slow checkout processing
- Poor reporting and decision-making
- Human errors in billing and stock updates

The store requires an integrated system to automate and centralize these operations.

## Business Case
The Retail Clothing Store Management System will:

### Improve Operational Efficiency
- Faster sales processing through a Point-of-Sale (POS) module
- Automatic inventory updates during sales

### Improve Inventory Accuracy
- Real-time stock tracking
- Low-stock alerts

### Enhance Decision-Making
- Sales reports (daily, weekly, monthly)
- Stock movement analysis

### Reduce Costs
- Minimize human errors
- Reduce stock losses
- Reduce time spent on manual reporting

### Business Value
- Increased profitability
- Better customer service
- Competitive advantage

The system provides clear operational and financial benefits, justifying investment.
